# בית המקדש השני — דוח דיוק ופערים
# Second Beit HaMikdash — Accuracy & Gap Analysis

Cross-referenced against: Mishnayot Middot (Ch. 1-5), Jewish Encyclopedia (Temple Plan of Second), Josephus (Antiquities 15:11, War of the Jews), Rambam (Hilchot Beit HaBechirah), and [beishamikdosh.com](https://www.beishamikdosh.com/3d-models.html).

---

## 1. הר הבית — TEMPLE MOUNT

### What we have:
- Rectangular platform (110 x 90 units)
- 4 retaining walls with stone texture
- Ground + surrounding hills

### What's WRONG:
- **Shape**: Har HaBayit was 500 x 500 cubits — a SQUARE, not a rectangle. Our model is rectangular (110x90)
- **Elevation**: The mount was lowest on the east side and rose gradually to its highest on the west. Our platform is flat
- **Steps**: 39 steps of 1/2 cubit rise + 1 step of 1 cubit (total 20.5 cubits elevation) led from the east up to the Heichal level. We only have a few steps

### What's MISSING:
| Structure | Status | Source |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Royal Stoa (Stoa Basilica)** — massive colonnade along the entire south wall, 162 columns in 4 rows | MISSING | Josephus, Antiquities 15:11 |
| **Solomon's Porch** — colonnade along the east wall | MISSING | Josephus |
| **Colonnades** on north and west walls | MISSING | Josephus |
| **Huldah Gates (2)** — south wall, one entrance one exit | MISSING | Middot 1:3 |
| **Shushan Gate** — east wall, with image of Susa palace | We have 1 generic gate | Middot 1:3 |
| **Tadi Gate** — north wall, triangular (no lintel!) | MISSING | Middot 1:3 |
| **Kiponus Gate** — west wall | MISSING | Middot 1:3 |
| **Mikvot (ritual baths)** — near southern entrance | MISSING | Archaeological evidence |

- **Gate size**: Nikanor Gate was the largest — 20 cubits wide, 40 cubits high. Our gate is tiny

### What's MISSING:
| Structure | Description | Source |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **Chamber of Pinchas** | Right side of Nikanor Gate — the vestment keeper stored priestly garments here | Middot 1:4 |
| **Chamber of Griddle Cake Makers** | Left side — prepared the Chavitin (daily griddle cakes for the Kohen Gadol) | Middot 1:4 |
| **The bronze doors themselves** | Two massive Corinthian bronze doors — should look magnificent | Middot 2:3 |
| **Music chambers** | Under the inner court, opening into the staircase — for the Levites | Jewish Encyclopedia |

## 6. המזבח — THE ALTAR

### What we have:
- 3-tier stepped structure with stone texture
- 4 horns (keranot)
- Ramp on south side
- Fire on top

### What's WRONG:
- **Position**: The altar should be centered in the Azarah, positioned exactly in front of the Heichal entrance. In our model it's at position (2,0,0) which may not be centered correctly
- **Ramp direction**: The Kevesh (ramp) was on the SOUTH side, 32 cubits long. Ours points in the Z direction which may be wrong depending on orientation
- **Red line (Chut HaSikra)**: A red painted line ran around the middle of the altar dividing upper and lower bloods. Missing

### What's MISSING:
| Structure | Description | Source |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **Yesod (foundation)** | Extended base on the north and west sides only (not all 4 sides) | Middot 3:1 |
| **Sovev (surrounding ledge)** | Walkway around the altar at the middle level where Kohanim walked | Middot 3:1 |
| **Chut HaSikra** | Red line around the middle of the altar | Middot 3:1 |
| **Drainage hole** | Southwest corner — blood drained to Nachal Kidron | Middot 3:2 |
| **Two tables at ramp base** | 1 marble (for placing limbs), 1 silver (for vessels) | Shekalim 6:4 |
| **3 fire arrangements on top** | Large pile, second pile for coals, third pile for incense | Tamid 2:4 |

## 7. הכיור — THE KIYOR (LAVER)

### What we have:
- Bronze base, basin, water surface

### What's WRONG:
- **Position**: Should be between the Ulam and the Altar, slightly to the south. Ours is at (2, 0, -7) which puts it SW of altar
- **The Muchni mechanism**: The Kiyor was lowered into a cistern at night (to keep water fresh) via a wooden mechanism called "Muchni ben Tin". Missing entirely

### What's MISSING:
| Structure | Description | Source |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **12 spigots** | For 12 Kohanim to wash simultaneously | Yoma 37a |
| **Muchni mechanism** | Wooden wheel device to lower/raise the Kiyor | Yoma 37a |

## 8. האולם — THE ULAM (Vestibule)

### What we have:
- Front wall, two side walls, gold-accented top
- Floor

### What's WRONG:
- **Dimensions**: Ulam facade should be 100 cubits wide x 100 cubits tall — the tallest structure. Our Ulam width is only 16 units and height 20 units (too small relative to the Heichal)
- **Depth**: Should be 11 cubits deep. Ours is only 4 units
- **No entrance opening**: The Ulam had a HUGE open entrance (20 cubits wide, 40 cubits high) with an embroidered curtain. Our model has a solid back wall with no opening

### What's MISSING:
| Structure | Description | Source |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **Embroidered curtain** at entrance | Instead of doors — richly decorated Parochet | Middot 3:4 |
| **5 superimposed carved oak beams** | Above the entrance, each extending 1 cubit further than the one below | Middot 3:4 |
| **2 marble tables** | Right: for placing new showbread. Left: for old showbread | Shekalim 6:4 |
| **Golden vine** | Suspended from cedar beams — donors hung gold "grapes" on it | Middot 3:5 |
| **Golden chains** | Hung from ceiling — young Kohanim climbed them to see the crowns in the windows | Middot 3:5 |
| **2 Chambers of Knives (Bet Chalifot)** | One on each side of the Ulam, 11x15x8 cubits each | Middot 4:7 |

## 9. ההיכל — THE HEICHAL (Sanctuary)

### What we have:
- 4 walls, floor, gold roof
- Menorah (south), Shulchan (north), Incense Altar (center)
- Gold wall panels
- Parochet (curtain separating from Kodesh HaKodashim)

### What's WRONG:
- **Dimensions**: Should be 20 x 40 cubits, 40 cubits high (our Heichal is 10x14x16 — proportionally shorter)
- **Entrance**: Should have a 10 x 20 cubit entrance with a DOUBLE DOOR — not open as in our model
- **Walls**: Should be lined with cedar wood overlaid with gold — our model uses stone texture on walls, should show gold/wood paneling
- **Additional tables and menorot**: Solomon added 5 extra tables on each side and 5 extra menorot on each side (10 total + original). We only have the originals

### What's MISSING:
| Structure | Description | Source |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **Double door at entrance** | 10 x 20 cubits | Middot 4:1 |
| **10 additional tables** | 5 on north, 5 on south (Solomon's addition) | Jewish Encyclopedia |
| **10 additional menorot** | 5 on each side (Solomon's addition) | Jewish Encyclopedia |
| **38 surrounding cells** | 15 on north (3 rows of 5), 15 on south, 8 on west (3 rows) — used for storage/defense | Middot 4:3-4 |
| **Windows near the top** | The Heichal had windows | Middot 4 |
| **Winding passage (Mesibah)** | 3 cubits wide, between the cells and outer wall | Middot 4:5 |
| **Upper story (Aliyah)** | Same dimensions as lower Heichal (40 cubits high), making total 100 cubits | Middot 4:6 |
| **Golden roof spikes** | To deter birds — we have some but they should be more prominent | Middot 4:6 |

## 10. קודש הקודשים — KODESH HAKODASHIM (Holy of Holies)

### What we have:
- 4 walls, floor, gold roof
- Even HaShtiyah (Foundation Stone)
- Glow light
- 1 Parochet

### What's WRONG:
- **Dimensions**: Should be 20 x 20 cubits (a perfect square), 40 cubits high. Ours is 8x8 (too small relative to Heichal)
- **TWO curtains**: There were TWO Parochet curtains with a 1 cubit gap between them (the "Amah Teraksin" — the cubit of doubt). We only have one

### What's MISSING:
| Structure | Description | Source |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **Second Parochet** | Two curtains, outer folded right, inner folded left, 1 cubit gap between | Middot, Yoma 51b |
| **Even HaShtiyah accuracy** | Should protrude only 3 thumb-breadths (~6 inches) above the floor. Ours is too tall | Yoma 53b |

## 12. PROPORTION & SCALE ISSUES

The biggest systematic problem is that our model does not maintain correct proportions:

| Part | Should be (cubits) | Our model (units) | Ratio issue |
|------|-------------------|--------------------|-------------|
| Har HaBayit | 500 x 500 | 110 x 90 | Should be square |
| Ezrat Nashim | 135 x 135 | 18 x 24 | Should be square |
| Azarah | 135 x 187 | 24 x 24 | Should be rectangular (wider E-W) |
| Heichal | 20 x 40 x 40 | 10 x 14 x 16 | Too short |
| Kodesh HaKodashim | 20 x 20 x 40 | 8 x 8 x 16 | Too small |
| Ulam facade | 100 x 100 | 16 x 20 | WAY too small — should tower over everything |
| Altar | 32 x 32 base | 10 x 10 | Too small |
